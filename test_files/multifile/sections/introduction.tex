\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

This paper presents a novel approach to solving the classical optimization problem
in high-dimensional spaces. Our method builds on the foundational work of
Smith et al.~\cite{smith2020} and extends the framework proposed by
Jones~\cite{jones2019}.

\subsection{Background}
Let $f: \R^n \to \R$ be a continuously differentiable function. The optimization
problem we consider is:
\begin{equation}
    \min_{x \in \R^n} f(x) \quad \text{subject to} \quad g(x) \leq 0
    \label{eq:opt}
\end{equation}

\subsection{Contributions}
Our main contributions are:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item A new convergence proof for gradient descent with adaptive step sizes
    \item An efficient implementation achieving $O(n \log n)$ complexity
    \item Extensive numerical experiments on benchmark datasets
\end{enumerate}

\begin{theorem}[Main Result]
\label{thm:main}
Let $f$ be $L$-smooth and $\mu$-strongly convex. Then the iterates $\{x_k\}$
generated by Algorithm~1 satisfy:
\[
    \norm{x_k - x^*}^2 \leq \left(1 - \frac{\mu}{L}\right)^k \norm{x_0 - x^*}^2
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The proof follows from the standard descent lemma combined with strong convexity.
See Section~\ref{sec:methods} for the complete derivation.
\end{proof}
